FAQ

Short answers to common support questions.

Why did the nudge get stronger?

Because DEADSCROLL is designed to escalate when the same pattern keeps returning. The first pause is not meant to be the last available intervention.

What if a permission gets revoked?

Core intervention behavior may stop working until that permission is restored in Android settings.

Is DEADSCROLL a pure blocker?

No. The product is built around contextual nudges and escalation, not a single universal lockout rule.

Can I choose which apps get nudged?

Yes. DEADSCROLL is meant to help you protect attention where drift is costly, not apply the same intervention to every app on your phone.
