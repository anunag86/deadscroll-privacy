Why DeadScroll Exists

You open your phone for a minute. And an hour is gone.

For the average person, this is not a rare failure of willpower. Social media apps, short-content apps, and entertainment apps are designed to keep attention moving, refreshing, and returning. A quick check stretches into a longer session, and the lost time adds up quietly across ordinary days.

The Pattern

A small intention becomes a much longer session.

People often open one of these apps for a tiny reason, then get pulled into a loop they never consciously meant to keep following.

The Loop

Some products are built to keep drift frictionless.

Infinite feeds, autoplay, novelty, and easy re-entry make it simple for attention to slip from intention into repetition.

The Cost

The loss feels small in the moment and heavy in hindsight.

That is why the problem gets normalized. Time usually disappears in scattered minutes first, then shows up later as hours, habits, and a weaker sense of control.

Intervention Philosophy

Why DeadScroll nudges instead of blocking?

App blockers shut the door. DeadScroll tries to catch the moment before you walk through it. It starts with a pause, then keeps nudging stronger only if the same loop keeps forming.

Typical Blocker

Brute interruption

  • Usually relies on blunt lockout instead of contextual intervention.
  • Often treats every target app and every session the same way.
  • Can trigger reactance, workarounds, or quick disablement.
  • Can stop access temporarily without helping the user recover intention.
DeadScroll

Contextual intervention

  • Introduces a pause while intention is still recoverable.
  • Escalates only when drift keeps forming.
  • Uses different nudge types for different loop states.
  • Tries to build long-term cooperation, not just short-term compliance.
Value-First Monetization

DeadScroll should earn payment only after it has already helped.

DeadScroll is being shaped around a simple rule: no ads, no sale of personal data, and no paywall-first posture. The product should prove its value by saving you an hour before it asks for a subscription.
